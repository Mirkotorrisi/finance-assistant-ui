# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who want to understand their finances without friction. Primary context: someone sitting down to review their money — where it went, what patterns are emerging, whether they're on track. They interact primarily through a chat interface, asking questions in natural language, and expect the product to surface the right data without extra navigation.

The app is built to demo well (portfolio / broader audience) — so it must make a strong first impression on someone who may only spend 90 seconds with it, while remaining genuinely useful to someone who uses it weekly.

## Product Purpose

An AI-powered personal finance assistant. The user types a question; the LLM backend returns a schema-validated UI plan that renders real financial components (tables, charts, cards, forms) directly in the chat. A separate dashboard surface gives a persistent at-a-glance view of accounts, transactions, and spending patterns.

Success: a user asks "how much did I spend on food last month?" and gets an exact, readable, visually structured answer in seconds — not a redirect to a settings screen.

## Brand Personality

Smart, modern, capable.

The product is AI-forward, but it doesn't announce itself as an "AI product" — it simply performs at a higher level than expected. The intelligence should feel ambient and precise. Users should finish an interaction thinking "that was fast and right" before they think "that was an AI."

## Anti-references

- **Consumer banking apps**: Chase, Revolut, Monzo — green-money palettes, cartoon coins, approachable-but-dumb. This product knows more; it should look like it does.
- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: cream backgrounds, navy CTAs, illustration-heavy hero sections. Indistinguishable from every other B2B tool from 2019–2024.
- **Loud AI branding**: gradient text, glowing orbits, sparkle icons, purple-to-teal everywhere. The AI is the mechanism, not the aesthetic.

## Design Principles

1. **Intelligence through precision** — every number, label, and data point should feel exact. Rounded corners and soft type are fine; vague or ambiguous information is not.
2. **Chat is the UI** — the conversational surface is the product's primary differentiator. Interaction patterns, component rendering, and visual hierarchy should all serve the conversation — not distract from it.
3. **Show depth on demand** — the product knows a lot. Surface a clean summary first; let the user drill in. Progressive disclosure over front-loading every metric.
4. **Restraint earns attention** — in a finance context, calm and controlled is more impressive than energetic. Visual emphasis should be earned by data importance, not decoration.
5. **Demo-legible at a glance** — someone unfamiliar with the product should understand what they're looking at within 10 seconds. The first screen should communicate "AI + finance + real data" without a tutorial.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components, full keyboard navigation, semantic HTML throughout.
